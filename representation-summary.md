## 1. Practice Before the IRS

### Definition of "Practice"

**Practice before the IRS** encompasses all matters connected with a presentation to the IRS relating to a taxpayer's rights, privileges, or liabilities under laws or regulations administered by the IRS. This includes:

- Preparing documents
- Filing documents
- Corresponding and communicating with the IRS
- Rendering written advice on federal tax matters
- Representing a client at conferences, hearings, and meetings

### Who Can Practice

#### Unlimited Practice Rights

| Practitioner | Enrollment/License | Authority | No Limitations |
|--------------|-------------------|-----------|----------------|
| **Enrolled Agent (EA)** | Pass SEE exam OR 5 years IRS experience | Enrolled by IRS | Any taxpayer, any matter, any IRS office |
| **Certified Public Accountant (CPA)** | State CPA license in good standing | Licensed by state | Any taxpayer, any matter, any IRS office |
| **Attorney** | State bar admission in good standing | Licensed by state | Any taxpayer, any matter, any IRS office |

#### Limited Practice Rights

| Practitioner | Authorization | Can Represent | Cannot Represent |
|--------------|--------------|---------------|------------------|
| **Annual Filing Season Program (AFSP)** | Annual IRS certification | Before revenue agents and customer service | Appeals, Collections, Counsel |
| **Unenrolled Return Preparer** | None required | Before revenue agents and customer service | Appeals, Collections, Counsel |
| **Enrolled Retirement Plan Agent (ERPA)** | IRS enrollment | Retirement plan matters only | Other tax matters |
| **Enrolled Actuary** | Joint Board enrollment | Actuarial matters only | Other tax matters |
| **Student (LITC/VITA)** | Special program authorization | Within program only | Outside program |
| **Immediate Family Member** | Relationship | Individual taxpayers | Partnerships, corporations |

### Where "Limited" Practitioners Can Appear

Limited practitioners (AFSP, unenrolled preparers) may ONLY represent taxpayers before:

1. **Revenue Agents** - During examinations of returns they prepared
2. **Customer Service Representatives** - For inquiries about returns they prepared

They **CANNOT** represent taxpayers before:
- IRS Appeals
- IRS Collections
- IRS Counsel
- Any office regarding returns they did not prepare

### Becoming an Enrolled Agent

**Two Paths to Enrollment:**

| Path | Requirements | Process |
|------|--------------|---------|
| **Examination** | Pass all 3 parts of SEE | Apply for enrollment within 2 years of passing last part |
| **IRS Experience** | 5+ years continuous IRS employment | Must have interpreted and applied tax law |

**Maintaining Enrollment:**
- Renew every 3 years
- Complete 72 hours of continuing education per 3-year cycle
- Minimum 16 hours per year
- 2 hours of ethics per year

## 2. Power of Attorney - Form 2848

### Overview

Form 2848, Power of Attorney and Declaration of Representative, authorizes an individual to represent a taxpayer before the IRS. This is the most comprehensive authorization form.

### What Form 2848 Authorizes

| Authorized Acts | Specifically Authorized Acts (Must Check Box) |
|-----------------|---------------------------------------------|
| Receive confidential tax information | Receive refund checks |
| Perform any act taxpayer could perform | Sign tax returns (limited circumstances) |
| Represent before all IRS offices | Substitute another representative |
| Execute consents to extend SOL | Sign closing agreements |
| Sign written agreements (not returns) | Endorse refund checks |

### Completing Form 2848

#### Line-by-Line Instructions

| Line | Content | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **Line 1** | Taxpayer information | Name, address, SSN/EIN, phone |
| **Line 2** | Representative information | Name, CAF#, PTIN, designation |
| **Line 3** | Tax matters | Form type, years/periods, specific matters |
| **Line 4** | Specific authorizations | Check boxes for special acts |
| **Line 5** | Retention/revocation | Prior POAs revoked unless specified |
| **Line 6** | Taxpayer signature | REQUIRED - must be signed |
| **Part II** | Representative declaration | Signed under penalties of perjury |

### Representative Designations (Line 2)

| Code | Designation | Requirements |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| a | Attorney | State bar member |
| b | CPA | State licensed |
| c | Enrolled Agent | IRS enrolled |
| d | Officer | Bona fide officer of taxpayer entity |
| e | Full-time employee | Regular full-time employee |
| f | Family member | Spouse, parent, child, sibling, etc. |
| g | Enrolled Actuary | Joint Board enrolled |
| h | Unenrolled Return Preparer | Prepared return being examined |
| k | ERPA | IRS enrolled |

### CAF Number System

**Centralized Authorization File (CAF)**
- Unique 9-digit number assigned to representatives
- Used to track all authorizations
- Same number used for all clients
- Request by submitting Form 2848

### Duration and Revocation

| Aspect | Rule |
|--------|------|
| **Duration** | Valid until revoked OR 7 years from signature date |
| **New POA** | Automatically revokes prior POAs for same matters unless Line 6 specifies retention |
| **Taxpayer Revocation** | Written statement to IRS or new POA |
| **Representative Withdrawal** | Written statement to IRS |
| **Death** | POA terminates upon taxpayer death |

### Submitting Form 2848

| Method | Contact |
|--------|---------|
| **Fax** | 855-214-7519 (most locations) |
| **Mail** | IRS submission processing center |
| **Submit with tax matter** | Attach to correspondence |
| **Online** | Tax Pro Account (limited) |

## 3. Tax Information Authorization - Form 8821

### Overview

Form 8821, Tax Information Authorization, allows a taxpayer to authorize any individual or organization to receive and inspect confidential tax information. **It does NOT authorize representation.**

### Form 8821 vs. Form 2848

| Feature | Form 8821 (TIA) | Form 2848 (POA) |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Receive tax info | Yes | Yes |
| Inspect returns | Yes | Yes |
| Represent taxpayer | **NO** | Yes |
| Speak for taxpayer | **NO** | Yes |
| Sign documents | **NO** | Yes |
| Receive refunds | **NO** | Yes (if authorized) |
| CAF number required | No | Yes |
| Duration | Until revoked | Until revoked or 7 years |

### When to Use Form 8821

- Third party needs information but NOT representation
- Mortgage company needs transcripts
- Bank needs proof of income
- Attorney needs info for non-tax matter
- Anyone who just needs to view information

### What Form 8821 Authorizes

**CAN:**
- Receive copies of notices
- Inspect tax information
- Receive verbal information from IRS
- Access transcripts and account information

**CANNOT:**
- Represent taxpayer before IRS
- Speak on taxpayer's behalf
- Sign any documents for taxpayer
- Receive refund checks
- Negotiate or agree to anything

## 4. Circular 230 Complete Guide

### Structure of Circular 230

| Subpart | Title | Content |
|---------|-------|---------|
| **A** | Rules Governing Authority to Practice | Who can practice, enrollment, limited practice |
| **B** | Duties and Restrictions | Practitioner obligations, prohibited conduct |
| **C** | Sanctions for Violation | Censure, suspension, disbarment, penalties |
| **D** | Rules for Disciplinary Proceedings | OPR procedures, hearings, appeals |
| **E** | General Provisions | Definitions, effective date |

### Subpart B: Duties and Restrictions

#### Duties Owed to Clients

| Duty | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Competence** | Must have necessary knowledge and skill |
| **Diligence** | Must pursue matters promptly and thoroughly |
| **Return Records** | Must return client records upon request |
| **Reasonable Fees** | Cannot charge unconscionable fees |
| **No Conflicts** | Cannot represent conflicting interests |
| **Written Fee Agreements** | Required for contingent fees |

#### Duties Owed to the IRS

| Duty | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Accurate Documents** | All submissions must be correct and complete |
| **No Delay** | Cannot unreasonably delay proceedings |
| **Assist Compliance** | Help clients comply with lawful requests |
| **Due Diligence** | Must verify accuracy of submissions |
| **No Misleading** | Cannot make false or misleading statements |
| **Inform of Errors** | Must advise client of errors and omissions |

#### Prohibited Conduct

| Prohibition | Explanation |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Negotiating Refund Checks** | Cannot endorse or negotiate client refund checks |
| **False Advertising** | No false or misleading statements about services |
| **Improper Solicitation** | Cannot use coercion or harassment |
| **Contingent Fees** | Prohibited for most original returns |
| **Practicing While Suspended** | Absolutely prohibited |
| **Assisting Disbarred** | Cannot help suspended practitioners practice |
| **Notarized Documents** | Cannot notarize documents for client representation |

### Knowledge of Client's Error or Omission

When a practitioner knows of a client's error or omission:

1. **Must** promptly advise client of the error
2. **Must** advise client of the consequences
3. **Must** advise client of the corrective measures
4. **Cannot** prepare current return ignoring known error
5. **Cannot** sign return with known unreported income

### Standards for Written Tax Advice

All written advice must:

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Be based on reasonable factual assumptions | Cannot rely on unreasonable assumptions |
| Consider all relevant facts | Must research thoroughly |
| Relate law to facts | Apply proper legal analysis |
| Not rely unreasonably on representations | Verify questionable representations |
| Consider relevant legal authorities | Research applicable law |

## 5. Due Diligence Requirements

### Credits and Statuses Requiring Due Diligence

| Credit/Status | Code Section | Penalty (2024) |
|---------------|--------------|----------------|
| Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) | §6695(g) | $635 |
| Child Tax Credit (CTC/ACTC) | §6695(g) | $635 |
| American Opportunity Tax Credit (AOTC) | §6695(g) | $635 |
| Other Dependent Credit (ODC) | §6695(g) | $635 |
| Head of Household (HOH) Filing Status | §6695(g) | $635 |

### The Four Due Diligence Requirements

#### Requirement 1: Complete and Submit Form 8867

- **Paid Preparer's Due Diligence Checklist**
- Must complete for EACH credit/status claimed
- Must be submitted with return

#### Requirement 2: Complete Applicable Worksheets

- EIC Worksheet
- CTC/ACTC Worksheet
- AOTC Worksheet
- Or alternative computation showing eligibility

#### Requirement 3: Make Reasonable Inquiries

| Duty | Actions |
|------|---------|
| Ask necessary questions | Document all client answers |
| Verify reasonableness | Compare to prior years, norms |
| Cannot ignore inconsistencies | Must resolve red flags |
| Document everything | Keep notes of all inquiries |

#### Requirement 4: Retain Records for 3 Years

Must retain:
- Copy of Form 8867
- Applicable worksheets
- Records of client responses
- Documentation supporting eligibility

### EITC Due Diligence Specifics

Must verify:
- Valid SSN for taxpayer, spouse, and qualifying children
- Age requirements (at least 25, under 65 if no QC)
- Principal residence in US for more than half the year
- Filing status requirements
- AGI limits
- Investment income limit ($11,000 for 2024)
- Cannot be qualifying child of another

### CTC Due Diligence Specifics

Must verify:
- Qualifying child definition met
- Child under age 17 at end of tax year
- Child has required SSN
- Relationship test
- Residency test (more than half year)
- Support test
- Taxpayer's AGI within limits

### AOTC Due Diligence Specifics

Must verify:
- Student enrolled at least half-time
- First four years of postsecondary education
- Student not claimed for 4 prior tax years
- No felony drug conviction
- Qualified tuition and related expenses
- AGI within limits

### HOH Due Diligence Specifics

Must verify:
- Unmarried (or considered unmarried) at end of year
- Paid more than half cost of keeping up home
- Qualifying person lived with taxpayer more than half year
- (Exception: dependent parent need not live with taxpayer)

### Penalty Calculation

**Penalty is $635 PER credit/status PER failure:**

| Scenario | Penalty |
|----------|---------|
| Return claims EITC, failed DD | $635 |
| Return claims EITC + CTC, failed DD for both | $1,270 |
| Return claims EITC + CTC + AOTC + HOH, failed all | $2,540 |

## 6. Standards for Tax Advice

### Standards for Tax Return Positions

#### Undisclosed Positions

| Standard | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| **General Rule** | Position must have **substantial authority** |
| **Tax Shelters** | Position must be **more likely than not** correct |

#### Disclosed Positions

| Standard | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| **With Disclosure** | Position must have **reasonable basis** |
| **Form 8275** | Use to disclose positions |

### Hierarchy of Authority

From highest to lowest:

1. **Internal Revenue Code** - Statutory law
2. **Treasury Regulations** - Official interpretations
3. **Revenue Rulings** - IRS official position
4. **Revenue Procedures** - IRS procedures
5. **Private Letter Rulings** - Specific taxpayer (not precedent)
6. **Tax Court Decisions** - Court interpretations
7. **Other courts** - District, Claims, Circuit
8. **IRS Publications** - General guidance (not authority)

### Substantial Authority

- **More than reasonable basis**
- **Less than more-likely-than-not**
- Approximately **40% likelihood** of being sustained
- Based on weight of authorities

### Reasonable Basis

- **Not frivolous**
- Approximately **20% likelihood** of being sustained
- Must be arguable position
- Requires good faith legal analysis

### More Likely Than Not

- **Greater than 50%** likelihood of being sustained
- Required for tax shelter positions
- Highest standard

### Reliance on Information from Clients

**Practitioner MAY rely on client information if:**
- Information is reasonable
- Does not appear incorrect or incomplete
- Does not conflict with known facts

**Practitioner CANNOT ignore:**
- Information that appears incorrect
- Inconsistencies in client statements
- Known facts that contradict client claims

## 7. Client Record Obligations

### What Are Client Records?

**Client records include:**
- Original documents provided by client
- Tax returns and supporting schedules
- Correspondence with IRS about client
- Notes and documentation of advice given
- Work papers to the extent they include client-provided information

**NOT client records (practitioner work product):**
- Practitioner's analysis and research
- Internal memos and strategy documents
- Proprietary methods or software output

### Return of Client Records

#### General Rule

A practitioner **must** return client records upon request, regardless of:
- Disputes over fees
- Outstanding balances
- Disagreements with client

#### Exception: Fee Dispute

Practitioners **may** retain copies of work papers pending resolution of fee disputes, but:
- Must return original documents immediately
- Must return documents necessary for client to comply with tax obligations
- State law may impose additional requirements

#### Timing

Records must be returned **promptly** upon request - generally within a reasonable time (typically 5-10 business days).

### Record Retention Requirements

| Record Type | Retention Period |
|-------------|------------------|
| Copy of return or list | 3 years from close of return period |
| Due diligence records (Form 8867) | 3 years from filing date |
| Consent to disclosure | 3 years from date consent signed |
| Client files | Per state law (typically 5-7 years) |

## 8. Fees and Advertising Rules

### Fee Rules

#### Prohibited Fees

| Prohibited | Reason |
|------------|--------|
| **Unconscionable fees** | Excessive based on services provided |
| **Contingent fees for original returns** | Creates incentive for aggressive positions |
| **Fee based solely on refund percentage** | For original returns |

#### Permitted Contingent Fees

Contingent fees ARE allowed for:
- Amended returns (claims for refund)
- IRS examination representation
- Claims for credit or refund filed after IRS examination has begun
- Interest and penalty abatement requests
- Judicial proceedings

#### Fee Disclosure

- Written fee agreements recommended
- Required for contingent fees
- Must explain basis for fee calculation

### Advertising Rules

#### Permitted Advertising

| Permitted | Notes |
|-----------|-------|
| Professional designation | EA, CPA, Attorney |
| Areas of practice | Tax preparation, representation |
| Fee information | If accurate |
| Languages spoken | As applicable |
| Contact information | Address, phone, website |

#### Prohibited Advertising

| Prohibited | Reason |
|------------|--------|
| False or misleading statements | Circular 230 violation |
| Claims of special connections | Cannot claim IRS preference |
| Guarantees of results | Cannot guarantee outcomes |
| Improper use of IRS name | Cannot suggest IRS endorsement |
| Coercive solicitation | Cannot harass potential clients |

### Solicitation Rules

| Method | Permitted? | Restrictions |
|--------|------------|--------------|
| Written mail | Yes | Must be labeled "advertisement" |
| Email | Yes | Must be labeled "advertisement" |
| In-person (non-prohibited) | Yes | Cannot use coercion |
| Phone calls | Limited | Cannot be harassing |
| After disaster/death | Prohibited for 30 days | Cannot solicit victims |

## 9. Disciplinary Proceedings

### Office of Professional Responsibility (OPR)

**OPR Authority:**
- Investigate practitioner conduct
- Institute disciplinary proceedings
- Negotiate settlements
- Recommend sanctions

### Grounds for Discipline

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Incompetence/Disreputable Conduct** | Conviction of crime, dishonesty, willful violations |
| **Violation of Circular 230** | Any provision violation |
| **Contemptuous Conduct** | Obstruction, disrespect to IRS |
| **False Statements** | Misleading IRS or clients |
| **Assisting Violations** | Helping others violate Circular 230 |

### Disciplinary Process Steps

| Step | Action | Timeframe |
|------|--------|-----------|
| 1 | Complaint received | Ongoing |
| 2 | OPR investigation | Varies |
| 3 | Notice of Institution of Proceedings | After investigation |
| 4 | Practitioner answer | 30 days |
| 5 | Pre-hearing conference | As scheduled |
| 6 | Hearing before ALJ | As scheduled |
| 7 | ALJ decision | After hearing |
| 8 | Appeal to Secretary of Treasury | 30 days |
| 9 | Federal court review (limited) | As applicable |

### Types of Sanctions

| Sanction | Definition | Effect on Practice |
|----------|------------|-------------------|
| **Censure** | Public reprimand | Can continue to practice |
| **Suspension** | Temporary bar | Cannot practice during period |
| **Disbarment** | Indefinite bar | Cannot practice; may petition reinstatement after 5 years |
| **Monetary Penalty** | Financial sanction | Up to 100% of gross income from engagement |

### Expedited Suspension

**Immediate suspension without full hearing when:**
- Practitioner convicted of tax-related crime
- Practitioner lost professional license (CPA, attorney)
- Practitioner poses threat to IRS or public
- Practitioner fails to comply with subpoena

### Reinstatement After Disbarment

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Waiting period | 5 years minimum |
| Application | Petition to OPR |
| Showing required | Competent, not threat to IRS |
| Decision | Within 180 days |

## 10. Preparer Penalties

### IRC §6694: Understatement Penalties

#### §6694(a) - Unreasonable Positions

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Trigger** | Position lacks substantial authority (or reasonable basis if disclosed) |
| **Penalty** | Greater of $1,000 or 50% of income derived from return |
| **Defense** | Reasonable cause and good faith |

#### §6694(b) - Willful or Reckless Conduct

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Trigger** | Willful attempt to understate OR reckless/intentional disregard |
| **Penalty** | Greater of $5,000 or 75% of income derived from return |
| **Defense** | None - strict liability |

### IRC §6695: Other Preparer Penalties

| Section | Violation | Penalty | Maximum |
|---------|-----------|---------|---------|
| 6695(a) | Failure to furnish copy to taxpayer | $60 | $30,000/year |
| 6695(b) | Failure to sign return | $60 | $30,000/year |
| 6695(c) | Failure to furnish PTIN | $60 | $30,000/year |
| 6695(d) | Failure to retain copy or list | $60 | $30,000/year |
| 6695(e) | Failure to file correct information return | $60 | $30,000/year |
| 6695(f) | Negotiating refund check | $600 | No maximum |
| 6695(g) | Due diligence failure | $635 | No maximum |

### Defenses to Preparer Penalties

| Penalty | Available Defense |
|---------|-------------------|
| §6694(a) | Reasonable cause and good faith |
| §6694(b) | None |
| §6695(a)-(e) | Reasonable cause (not willful neglect) |
| §6695(f) | None |
| §6695(g) | Reasonable cause (documented) |

## 11. Innocent Spouse Relief

### Three Types of Relief Under §6015

#### Innocent Spouse Relief - §6015(b)

| Requirement | Must Show |
|-------------|-----------|
| Joint return filed | Must have been joint return |
| Understatement of tax | Due to erroneous items of other spouse |
| No knowledge | Did not know and had no reason to know |
| Inequitable | Inequitable to hold liable |
| Timely request | Within 2 years of first collection action |

#### Separation of Liability - §6015(c)

| Requirement | Must Show |
|-------------|-----------|
| Joint return filed | Must have been joint return |
| No longer married or living apart | Divorced, legally separated, or lived apart 12+ months |
| Understatement of tax | Only understatement allocated, not underpayment |
| Timely request | Within 2 years of first collection action |

#### Equitable Relief - §6015(f)

| Requirement | Must Show |
|-------------|-----------|
| Does not qualify for (b) or (c) | Other relief unavailable |
| Inequitable | Taking all facts into account |
| Can apply to underpayments | Unlike (b) and (c) |
| No specific time limit | But generally 2 years |

### Understatement vs. Underpayment

| Term | Definition | Relief Available |
|------|------------|------------------|
| **Understatement** | Tax shown on return is less than correct tax | §6015(b), (c), or (f) |
| **Underpayment** | Tax correctly reported but not paid | §6015(f) ONLY |

### Injured Spouse - Form 8379

**Different from innocent spouse!**

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Situation** | Joint refund offset for OTHER spouse's debt |
| **Debts include** | Child support, federal debt, state debt |
| **Purpose** | Recover YOUR share of refund |
| **Form** | Form 8379 - Injured Spouse Allocation |
| **Timing** | File with return OR within 3 years |

### Relief Comparison Table

| Feature | Innocent §6015(b) | Separation §6015(c) | Equitable §6015(f) | Injured 8379 |
|---------|-------------------|--------------------|--------------------|--------------|
| Joint return | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Problem | Understatement | Understatement | Either | Offset |
| Knowledge | No knowledge | Any | Factor | N/A |
| Marital status | Any | Divorced/separated | Any | Any |
| Form | 8857 | 8857 | 8857 | 8379 |
| Time limit | 2 years | 2 years | Varies | 3 years |

## 12. Taxpayer Advocate Service

### Overview

The Taxpayer Advocate Service (TAS) is an **independent organization within the IRS** that helps taxpayers resolve problems and recommends changes to prevent future problems.

### When TAS Can Help

| Criteria | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Significant hardship** | Financial difficulty, business harm |
| **Immediate adverse action** | Imminent levy, lien, or seizure |
| **Cost exceeds benefit** | Time/expense outweighs tax at stake |
| **Delay over 30 days** | IRS has not responded timely |
| **Systemic problem** | Issue affects many taxpayers |

### How to Request TAS Assistance

| Method | Contact |
|--------|---------|
| **Form 911** | Request for Taxpayer Advocate Service Assistance |
| **Phone** | 877-777-4778 |
| **Fax** | Local TAS office |
| **Mail** | Local TAS office |
| **In Person** | Local TAS office |

### What TAS Can Do

- **Issue Taxpayer Assistance Orders (TAO)** - Stop IRS collection actions
- **Expedite case resolution** - Push IRS to act faster
- **Advocate with IRS offices** - Negotiate on taxpayer's behalf
- **Identify systemic problems** - Report to Congress
- **Protect taxpayer rights** - Enforce Taxpayer Bill of Rights

### Taxpayer Bill of Rights

| # | Right | Description |
|---|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Be Informed | Know what to do to comply |
| 2 | Quality Service | Prompt, courteous, professional assistance |
| 3 | Pay No More Than Correct Amount | Including interest and penalties |
| 4 | Challenge and Be Heard | Object and provide documentation |
| 5 | Appeal | Independent forum for disputes |
| 6 | Finality | Know when IRS is finished |
| 7 | Privacy | Only necessary intrusion |
| 8 | Confidentiality | Information not improperly disclosed |
| 9 | Retain Representation | Authorized representative |
| 10 | Fair and Just System | Consider facts and circumstances |
